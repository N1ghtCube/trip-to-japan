Tonkatsu

This dish consists of a pork covered with bread pieces deep-fried. In our case we cooked it ourselves on stone and had a lot of side-dishes as well. Most of the stuff came into the rice and sauces were used for meat. It was a nice, light meal with nothing extra-ordinary. Nothing stood out so it was pleasant to eat, and portion was super large so I couldn't finish it. We also tried our first miso soup there which was supposed to be drunk by itself, and it was like bouillon with seaweed.

Sashimi

We ordered bowl of rice and sashimi, and this fish was for sure fresh and tasty. My favourite was tuna since it didn't have any unpleasant taste at all.

Sushi

We went into Kura Sushi which is not a fancy restaurant but sushi there were good and we had no complaints about it. They didn't have as rich flavout as sashimi, but we just wanted to eat so it was alright.

Udon

This was the first time we tried a dish with such richness of flavour. Noodles were a bit too big to normally eat but they didn't loose their structure and were nice to consume. The meat was of a horse and I understood that I am not a big fan of it. Again, portion was very big.

Karaage

I got myself a bouillon with karaage which is deep-fried chiken and when I added it together I understood my mistake, because it quickly got soaky and wasn't really getting good together. But I still enjoyed the meal and again I got a lot of food.

Ramen

The bouillon was as rich as in udon, and I prefered more these noodles. I didn't quite like menma(marinated bamboo), but meat was good and all in all I enjoyed it.

Crab

Here I for the first time tried crab and it wasn't really much of a delicacy and since it's not cheap I probably will never eat it.

Curry

This dish was quite spicy, without rice it would be problematic to eat but still had a great taste. We had a choice of not adding chicken but it for sure tasted better with it.

Wagyuu

In my opinion, everyone should try this at least once. It was nothing like any previous beef I ate before. And even though we paid ~5 euro per each slice of meat it was totally worth it. I for the fist time experienced meat melting in my mouth. We coocked it ourselves, so I guess if professionals did it, it would be even better.

Macha

That was a pretty bitter tea and most of the time it was served in small portions. It is similar if you leave a bag of green tea stay for a long time in your cup.

Mochi

I honestly expected it to have more of the taste even though it's mostly rice, but still it was a nice sweet.

Oolong tea

That is the most unusual stuff we tried there, because it tastes like some volcanic water so we drank it only in restaurants where it was served for free.

Sake

I haven't tasted any alcohol before so I can just say that it didn't have any taste and only unpleasant feeling of alcohol could be felt.

Breakfast in hotel

In half of our hotels we had a buffet where we could choose our food and it was alright, some of it resembled european culture some of it japanese, and the only thing I can say is that they surely have troubles with fruits and a bit with meat, that is why it's hard to find it there.

Convenience food

In convenience stores you can find prepared food and unlike other contries it actually is fresh and tastes good, so for most of our dinners we would have that as our meal(we had lunches late and they were big).

Candies

I haven't tried a lot of them, but just from seeing there was a huge variety of them.

Vending machine

That thing is the most advanced there, since not only do they have a huge range of things you can buy from there, food also can come in different temperatures, that is why you can have a cold icecream or a drink, or hot tea and sandwich.
